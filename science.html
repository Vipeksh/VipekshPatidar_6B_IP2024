Gsat Satellite

Gsat-The GSAT (Geosynchronous Satellite) satellites are India's indigenously developed communications satellites, used for digital audio, data and video broadcasting.

20 GSAT satellites manufactured by ISRO have been launched, out of which 14 are in service.

NavIC Satellite

Navic-The Indian Regional Navigation Satellite System (IRNSS), with an operational name of NavIC (acronym for navic Navigation with Indian Constellation; also, navik 'sailor' or 'navigator' in Indian languages), is an autonomous regional satellite navigation system that provides accurate real-time positioning and timing services.


.

NavIC system consists of eight satellites, three of them in geostationary orbits and five in inclined geosynchronous orbits, with plans to expand to 12 satellites

Weather Prediction

Gsat

Gsat does not focus on weather predicition because of the following reasons:

  • GSAT satellites are equipped with transponders and payloads designed for telecommunication, broadcasting, and internet services.
  • Weather prediction requires specialized sensors like radiometers, spectrometers, and imaging systems (e.g., for infrared or microwave imaging) to monitor atmospheric conditions.
  • GSAT operates in a geostationary orbit, which is suitable for broad and constant communication coverage but not ideal for capturing high-resolution data for weather patterns.
  • GSAT is focused on signal transmission, which doesn't require detailed observation of Earth's atmosphere.
  • Weather prediction demands real-time data on parameters such as temperature, humidity, cloud cover, and wind patterns, which GSAT cannot provide.

Navigation

Gsat

  • GSAT satellites do not primarily focus on navigation. Their main purpose is communication, broadcasting, and internet services, rather than navigation.GSAT satellites are designed for telecommunications, television broadcasting, and internet connectivity, catering to the needs of India's communication infrastructure.
  • GSAT satellites like GSAT-8, GSAT-10, and GSAT-15 host transponders for GAGAN, an augmentation system that improves the accuracy of GPS signals for aviation and other high-precision applications.
  • This role is supportive, not central, to GSAT's overall mission.

Navic

  1. NavIC (Navigation with Indian Constellation) satellites are specifically designed to focus on navigation and positioning services.
  2. NavIC’s sole mission is to deliver reliable navigation services for both civilian and military applications in India and surrounding regions (up to 1,500 km).
  3. It provides positioning data similar to global systems like GPS (USA), Galileo (EU), and GLONASS (Russia).
  4. Each NavIC satellite carries navigation payloads that transmit signals in the L5-band and S-band frequencies for accurate positioning. The satellites also include an atomic clock, crucial for precise timing required in navigation systems.
  5. NavIC help in navigation for:Land Navigation: For vehicles, smartphones, and tracking systems.
    Maritime and Aviation: Assists in safe navigation in Indian waters and airspace. Disaster Management: Helps locate affected areas and plan rescue operations. Precision Agriculture: Enhances productivity through accurate mapping. Surveying and Mapping: Improves accuracy in construction and infrastructure projects.
  6. Unlike global systems like GPS, NavIC provides regional coverage, focusing on India and nearby areas, ensuring higher accuracy within this range.

Natural Disasater Management

Gsat

  • GSAT satellites do not primarily focus on Natural Disaster Management (NDM), but they play a supportive role in such scenarios due to their communication capabilities.
  • During disasters, terrestrial communication networks (like cell towers) can be disrupted.
  • Facilitates the transfer of disaster-related data and alerts from monitoring systems to ground stations and end users.
  • GSAT satellites indirectly support NDM by ensuring robust communication during emergencies, but they are not designed or focused on disaster management tasks.

Navic

  1. NavIC (Navigation with Indian Constellation) does not primarily focus on Natural Disaster Management (NDM), but it plays an important supporting role in NDM scenarios due to its accurate positioning and timing capabilities.
  2. NavIC is primarily a regional navigation system, designed to provide precise location and timing services for civilian and strategic applications within India and nearby regions.
  3. NavIC can transmit early warning alerts for natural disasters (e.g., cyclones, tsunamis) to devices equipped with NavIC receivers, such as: 1.Smartphones. 2.Maritime vessels in coastal regions.
  4. NavIC plays a supporting role in NDM by providing precise location, timing, and navigation services. While it is not explicitly designed for disaster management, it significantly enhances early warning systems, rescue operations, and response coordination.
